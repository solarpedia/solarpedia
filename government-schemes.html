Government Solar Schemes in India

Explore the latest central and state government solar schemes, rooftop solar subsidies, net metering policies, and financial assistance programs available for residential, commercial, agricultural, and industrial consumers across India.

Government Support for Solar Energy

The Government of India actively promotes renewable energy through several schemes designed to reduce electricity costs, increase clean energy adoption, and help households, farmers, businesses, and institutions install rooftop solar systems.

On this page, you'll find information about the most important solar schemes, eligibility criteria, benefits, and application procedures.

Popular Solar Schemes

🏠 PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana

A flagship Government of India initiative that provides financial assistance for residential rooftop solar installations, helping households reduce electricity bills and generate clean energy.

  • Residential rooftop solar subsidy
  • Lower electricity bills
  • Online application process
  • Central Government support
Learn More

🌾 PM-KUSUM Scheme

Supports farmers by promoting solar-powered irrigation pumps and decentralized renewable energy projects to reduce diesel usage and improve farm productivity.

  • Solar irrigation pumps
  • Support for farmers
  • Renewable energy adoption
  • Reduced fuel costs
Learn More

⚡ Net Metering

Net Metering allows excess electricity generated by your rooftop solar system to be exported to the grid, reducing your electricity bill.

  • Export excess electricity
  • Reduce monthly bills
  • Available in most states
  • Easy billing mechanism
Learn More

🏛 State Solar Subsidies

Several Indian states offer additional financial support, incentives, or policy benefits for rooftop solar installations beyond central government assistance.

  • State-wise incentives
  • Additional benefits
  • Renewable energy promotion
  • Residential & commercial support
Learn More

Who Can Apply?

  • Homeowners installing rooftop solar systems.
  • Residential housing societies.
  • Farmers eligible under PM-KUSUM.
  • Commercial and institutional consumers (scheme dependent).
  • Applicants meeting the DISCOM and government eligibility requirements.

How to Apply

  1. Choose the appropriate government solar scheme.
  2. Check your eligibility requirements.
  3. Select an approved solar vendor.
  4. Submit the required documents.
  5. Install the solar system.
  6. Inspection by the DISCOM.
  7. Receive subsidy (if applicable).

Frequently Asked Questions

Can every home receive a solar subsidy?

Eligibility depends on the scheme, installation type, and current government guidelines.

Is net metering available everywhere?

Net metering policies differ between states and local electricity distribution companies.

Can farmers receive special benefits?

Yes. PM-KUSUM specifically supports farmers through solar-powered irrigation and renewable energy initiatives.

Can I apply online?

Many government schemes provide online application facilities through official government portals or your electricity distribution company.
