How Solar Energy Works

Understand how sunlight is converted into electricity and how a rooftop solar system powers your home or business.

Introduction

Solar energy is one of the cleanest and most abundant renewable energy sources available. A solar power system captures sunlight using solar panels and converts it into usable electricity for homes, businesses, schools, and industries.

Understanding how solar energy works helps you make informed decisions when planning a rooftop solar installation and choosing the right equipment.

How Solar Electricity is Generated

☀ Step 1: Sunlight Reaches the Solar Panels

Solar panels absorb sunlight throughout the day. The more direct sunlight they receive, the more electricity they can produce.

🔋 Step 2: Solar Cells Generate Electricity

Each solar panel contains many photovoltaic (PV) cells. When sunlight strikes these cells, electrons begin to move, producing Direct Current (DC) electricity.

⚡ Step 3: DC Electricity is Produced

The electricity generated by solar panels is Direct Current (DC). Most household appliances cannot use DC electricity directly.

🔄 Step 4: Solar Inverter Converts DC to AC

A solar inverter converts DC electricity into Alternating Current (AC), which is the type of electricity used by homes and businesses.

🏠 Step 5: Power Your Home

The AC electricity is supplied to your home's electrical system, allowing lights, fans, TVs, refrigerators, air conditioners, and other appliances to operate using solar energy.

🔌 Step 6: Extra Electricity Goes to the Grid

If your solar system produces more electricity than your home consumes, the excess energy can be exported to the electricity grid through a net meter, subject to your DISCOM's regulations.

🌙 Step 7: Battery Storage (Optional)

If your system includes batteries, excess solar energy can be stored during the day and used at night or during power outages, depending on the system design.

Main Components of a Solar System

☀ Solar Panels

Capture sunlight and convert it into DC electricity.

🔄 Solar Inverter

Converts DC electricity into AC electricity for household use.

⚡ Net Meter

Measures both imported and exported electricity for eligible grid-connected systems.

🔋 Battery (Optional)

Stores excess electricity for later use.

🏗 Mounting Structure

Supports the solar panels and positions them for optimal sunlight exposure.

🛡 Protection Devices

Includes DCDB, ACDB, earthing, surge protection, and safety equipment to protect the solar installation.

Advantages of Solar Energy

  • Reduces your monthly electricity bills.
  • Uses clean and renewable energy from the sun.
  • Helps reduce carbon emissions and environmental pollution.
  • Requires relatively low maintenance after installation.
  • Increases the value of your property.
  • Can provide long-term savings over the system's lifetime.
  • May qualify for government subsidies and incentives, subject to applicable policies.

Types of Solar Power Systems

1. On-Grid Solar System

An on-grid solar system is connected to the electricity grid. Excess electricity can be exported through a net meter, while additional electricity can be imported from the grid when needed.

2. Off-Grid Solar System

An off-grid system operates independently of the electricity grid. It uses batteries to store energy and is suitable for remote locations without reliable grid access.

3. Hybrid Solar System

A hybrid solar system combines grid connectivity with battery storage. It allows you to use solar energy, store excess power, and draw electricity from the grid when necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can solar panels generate electricity on cloudy days?

Yes. Solar panels continue to generate electricity on cloudy days, although the output is usually lower than on bright sunny days.

Do solar panels work during a power cut?

Most standard on-grid systems automatically shut down during a power outage for safety reasons. Hybrid and off-grid systems with battery backup can continue supplying power, depending on their design.

How long do solar panels last?

Most high-quality solar panels have a lifespan of 25 years or more with proper maintenance.

Do solar panels require regular maintenance?

Maintenance is generally minimal. Periodic cleaning and routine inspections help maintain good performance.

Can I install solar panels on any roof?

Most roofs are suitable if they have adequate strength, sufficient sunlight exposure, and enough available space.
