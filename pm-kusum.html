PM-KUSUM Scheme

Learn about the PM-KUSUM Scheme, a Government of India initiative that promotes solar-powered agriculture through solar pumps and decentralized renewable energy projects.

What is PM-KUSUM?

PM-KUSUM (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan) is a Government of India scheme designed to encourage the use of solar energy in agriculture. The scheme helps farmers reduce dependence on diesel and conventional electricity by promoting solar-powered irrigation and renewable energy.

The scheme supports eligible farmers and agricultural entities through different components aimed at increasing clean energy use while improving access to reliable power for irrigation.

Quick Facts

🌾 Target Beneficiaries

Farmers, farmer groups, cooperatives, panchayats, and other eligible agricultural entities under the scheme.

☀️ Objective

Promote the use of solar energy in agriculture while reducing dependence on diesel and conventional electricity.

🏛 Implemented By

Government of India in coordination with State Governments and implementing agencies.

⚡ Main Focus

Solar pumps, decentralized solar power generation, and solarization of existing agricultural pumps.

PM-KUSUM Components

Component A

Supports the installation of decentralized, ground-mounted solar power plants on suitable land. These projects help generate renewable electricity closer to where it is consumed.

Component B

Supports eligible farmers in adopting standalone solar-powered agricultural pumps for irrigation where grid electricity is limited or unavailable.

Component C

Encourages the solarization of existing grid-connected agricultural pumps to reduce electricity consumption from conventional sources.

Benefits of PM-KUSUM

💰 Lower Irrigation Costs

Solar-powered pumps can reduce spending on diesel and help lower long-term irrigation costs.

🌱 Clean Energy

Using solar energy helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions and supports environmentally friendly farming.

⚡ Reliable Power

Solar pumps can provide dependable daytime electricity for irrigation, depending on sunlight availability.

🚜 Better Farm Productivity

Reliable access to irrigation can support agricultural activities and improve water management.

📈 Long-Term Savings

Although installation has an initial cost, solar systems can reduce operating expenses over many years.

🇮🇳 Supports Renewable Energy

The scheme contributes to India's clean energy goals by encouraging the use of solar power in agriculture.

Eligibility

  • Eligible individual farmers.
  • Groups of farmers.
  • Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs), where applicable.
  • Cooperatives and Panchayats, subject to current scheme guidelines.
  • Applicants meeting the eligibility criteria notified by the concerned authorities.

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card or other accepted identity proof.
  • Address proof.
  • Land ownership or land-use documents (if required).
  • Recent passport-size photograph.
  • Bank account details.
  • Mobile number.
  • Electricity connection details (for applicable components).

How to Apply

  1. Check your eligibility for the relevant PM-KUSUM component.
  2. Visit the official government portal or your State Nodal Agency.
  3. Complete the application form.
  4. Upload the required documents.
  5. Wait for verification by the concerned authority.
  6. After approval, install the solar system through the approved process.
  7. Inspection and commissioning are completed as per applicable guidelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can apply for PM-KUSUM?

Eligible farmers and other qualified agricultural entities can apply according to the latest government guidelines.

Does PM-KUSUM provide financial assistance?

Financial assistance depends on the applicable component and the latest government rules. Check the official scheme details before applying.

Can I install a solar pump under this scheme?

Depending on the applicable component and your eligibility, solar-powered agricultural pumps may be supported.

Where can I apply?

Applications are generally submitted through the official government portal or the State Nodal Agency responsible for implementing the scheme.

Can scheme rules change?

Yes. Eligibility, financial assistance, and implementation guidelines may change over time. Always refer to the latest official notifications.
